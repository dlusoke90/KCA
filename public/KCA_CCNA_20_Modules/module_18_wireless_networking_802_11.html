Core Concepts

802.11 Standards

802.11a: 5GHz, 54Mbps. 802.11b: 2.4GHz, 11Mbps. 802.11g: 2.4GHz, 54Mbps. 802.11n (WiFi 4): 2.4/5GHz, 600Mbps. 802.11ac (WiFi 5): 5GHz, 3.5Gbps. 802.11ax (WiFi 6): 2.4/5/6GHz, 9.6Gbps.

WiFi 4 (n): 600MbpsWiFi 5 (ac): 3.5GbpsWiFi 6 (ax): 9.6GbpsWiFi 6E: adds 6GHz
📻

Wireless Channels

2.4GHz: 11 channels (US), only 3 non-overlapping (1,6,11). 5GHz: more channels, less interference. 6GHz (WiFi 6E): even more spectrum. DFS channels avoid radar. Channel bonding: 40/80/160MHz width.

2.4GHz: 1,6,11 non-overlap5GHz: more channelsChannel bondingDFS avoids radar
🔒

Wireless Security

WEP: broken, never use. WPA: deprecated. WPA2 (802.11i): AES-CCMP, current standard. WPA3: SAE (Simultaneous Authentication of Equals), forward secrecy. Enterprise: 802.1X + RADIUS authentication.

WEP: broken ✗WPA: deprecated ✗WPA2: AES-CCMP ✓WPA3: SAE ✓ (best)
📡

Wireless Architecture

Autonomous AP: standalone, self-configured. Lightweight AP (LAP): managed by WLC (Wireless LAN Controller). CAPWAP: tunnel between AP and WLC. Split-MAC: control on WLC, data locally. Cisco DNA Center: modern management.

Autonomous: standaloneLightweight: WLC-managedCAPWAP tunnelWLC centralized
