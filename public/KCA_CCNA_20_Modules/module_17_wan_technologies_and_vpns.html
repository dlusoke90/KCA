WAN Technologies & VPNs

Core Concepts
🌐

WAN Connection Types

Leased Line (T1/E1): dedicated, expensive. MPLS: provider-managed, QoS. Metro Ethernet: high bandwidth. Broadband: DSL/Cable (home/small office). Cellular (4G/5G): mobile WAN. SD-WAN: software-defined.

Leased Line: dedicatedMPLS: provider-managedBroadband: DSL/CableSD-WAN: software-defined
🔒

VPN Types

Site-to-Site: permanent tunnel between sites. Remote Access: individual users connect. GRE: tunnel non-IP protocols, no encryption. IPsec: encryption + authentication. SSL/TLS VPN: browser-based, port 443.

Site-to-Site permanentRemote Access usersGRE: no encryptionIPsec: encrypted
🛡️

IPsec VPN

Two phases: IKE Phase 1 (ISAKMP SA - authenticate peers, negotiate encryption). IKE Phase 2 (IPsec SA - protect actual traffic). Protocols: AH (authentication only), ESP (auth + encryption). ESP preferred.

IKE Phase 1: authenticateIKE Phase 2: encrypt dataAH: auth onlyESP: auth + encrypt ✓
⚡

SD-WAN

Modern WAN approach. Centralized controller. Multiple WAN links (MPLS + broadband). Application-aware routing. ZTP (Zero Touch Provisioning). Cisco Viptela, VMware NSX, Meraki. Lower cost than MPLS.

Centralized controllerMultiple WAN linksApp-aware routingLower cost than MPLS
